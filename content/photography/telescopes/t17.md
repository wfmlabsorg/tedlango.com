## Overview

A dedicated science platform, normally used for deep photometry, narrowband, and extended-red IR imaging. Holds the world record for the most distant object imaged by an amateur. Can detect magnitude 21–22 galaxies in 300-second exposures. Features extended red sensitivity via deep depletion CCD. Not recommended as a pure imaging system for newcomers due to CCD sensitivity characteristics.

## Location

**Siding Spring Observatory** — Australia (MPC Q62)
