Onyx is the roaster I point people to when they say "I don't like coffee" — because what they don't like is usually dark, bitter, over-roasted coffee, and Onyx roasts about as far from that as you can get.

They're out of Rogers, Arkansas, which surprises people who assume specialty coffee only happens on a coast. They've built a reputation on **light, clean roasts** that chase clarity over comfort, a stack of barista-competition placings, and unusually transparent sourcing — they'll tell you the producer, the elevation, the process, and roughly what they paid.

**Why they're worth seeking out:**

- **They don't hide the bean behind the roast.** A washed Ethiopian from Onyx tastes like a washed Ethiopian, not like "coffee."
- **Naturals are a strength.** If you want to understand what natural processing does, their fruit-forward lots are a great teacher.
- **The blends are actually good.** *Geometry*, their flagship, is the rare blend designed to be forgiving across brew methods — a reasonable everyday bag while you learn.

**Ordering notes:** light roasts go stale faster than people expect — buy what you'll drink in 3–4 weeks, and give the bag a few days of rest off-roast before you judge it. Shipping from Arkansas, that rest usually happens in transit.
