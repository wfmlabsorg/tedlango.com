I bought *Geometry* as a control. Onyx's single origins are the showy ones, but I wanted a bag I could brew badly and still enjoy — something to practice on without wasting a $25 micro-lot.

It delivered exactly that. Where their Ethiopians are all jasmine and citrus, Geometry is **milk chocolate and brown sugar** with just enough dried-cherry brightness to keep it from going flat. It's a blend built to be robust across methods, and it is.

**How I brewed it:**

- **V60, 1:16, 96°C** — clean and sweet, chocolate forward, the fruit showing up as it cooled. My everyday cup.
- **Espresso (forgiving end of my range)** — pulled a touch long, it made a genuinely good cortado. Held up to milk better than a brighter single origin would.

**How it changed across the bag:** days 5–14 off-roast were the sweet spot. Right after it arrived it was a little tight and closed; by week three the aromatics had faded and it leaned flat. Lesson logged: even a forgiving blend has a window.

**Verdict / would-rebuy:** 8.0. Not the most exciting coffee I've had, but the one I reached for most. This is the bag I'd give someone who thinks they don't like coffee. Rebuy: yes, as a baseline — but I came away wanting to spend the next bag on one of their naturals to chase contrast.
