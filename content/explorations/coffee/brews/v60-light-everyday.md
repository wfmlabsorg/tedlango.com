This is the recipe I reach for nine mornings out of ten, dialed in on light and medium-light roasts. It's deliberately boring and repeatable — the point of an everyday recipe is that it removes variables so I can actually taste the *coffee*, not my own inconsistency.

**The parameters:**

| | |
|---|---|
| Dose | 18 g |
| Water | 288 g (**1:16**) |
| Grind | medium-fine — Comandante **22 clicks** |
| Water temp | 96 °C |
| Bloom | 50 g, 45 s |
| Pours | bloom → 180 g @ 0:45 → 288 g @ 1:20 |
| Total time | ~2:45 |

**Why these numbers.** Light roasts are dense and under-extract easily, so I run the water hot (96°C) and the grind a hair finer than people expect for V60. 1:16 is a sweet spot — enough strength to have body, enough water to pull the florals out. Three pours keep the bed even without me fussing over a dozen tiny pulses.

**Troubleshooting (the part worth memorizing):**

- **Sour / thin / sharp** = under-extracted → grind **finer**, water **hotter**, or slow the pours down. Start with grind.
- **Bitter / dry / hollow** = over-extracted → grind **coarser**, water slightly cooler, or speed up. If it's drawing down past ~3:00, go coarser.
- **Drawdown should finish around 2:45.** Way faster = too coarse; stalling past 3:15 = too fine or pouring too aggressively.

**Scaling.** It's ratio-based, so it just works at any size: keep **1:16** and recompute. 24 g → 384 g water, bloom ~60 g. 12 g → 192 g, bloom ~30 g. The grind and temp don't change; only the quantities do.
