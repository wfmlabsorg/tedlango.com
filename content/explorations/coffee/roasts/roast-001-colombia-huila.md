First real roast. I picked a washed Colombia Huila on purpose — it's forgiving, widely available green, and a known quantity, so any weirdness in the cup would be *me*, not the bean.

**What I was trying to do:** hit a clean medium-light, drop shortly after first crack with enough development that it isn't grassy or sour, but well short of second crack. Target development time ratio (DTR) around 15–20%.

**The batch:**

| | |
|---|---|
| Machine | SR800 + extension tube |
| Charge | 220 g green @ 200 °C |
| First crack | 8:30 |
| Second crack | — (didn't take it there) |
| Drop | 10:15 @ 210 °C |
| Development | 1:45 (**DTR ~17%**) |
| Cooling | colander toss, ~4 min |
| Rest | 7 days |

**What the senses told me.** First crack was later and quieter than the YouTube videos led me to expect — I almost missed it because I had the fan too high and it was drowning out the sound. Smell went from grassy → bready → that first hint of sweetness right around 9:30, which is where I started watching for the drop.

**What I got wrong.** Two things. (1) Fan too aggressive early, which stretched the time-to-first-crack and probably baked it slightly. (2) My cooling was slow — a colander toss takes too long, and the beans kept developing in residual heat after the "drop." The cup, after resting a week, was sweet and clean but a little flat and papery — classic mild baking.

**Next time, change ONE thing:** lower the fan in the first three minutes to build heat faster and get to first crack sooner, and rig a faster cooling setup (a second colander with a box fan). Everything else stays, so I can actually tell what that change did.

Resting note: at 7 days it was drinkable; honestly it was better at day 10. Washed Colombians seem to want more rest than I assumed.
