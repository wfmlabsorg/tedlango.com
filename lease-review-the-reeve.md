## CONCESSION CLAWBACK — READ THIS CAREFULLY

**This is the single most important clause in the lease.**

The Rent Concession Addendum (Page 4) states:

- You receive $8,397 in free rent (May, June, July 2026) plus a $211/mo monthly discount.
- **If the lease is terminated early due to default** (non-payment, eviction, abandonment), you must **immediately repay ALL concessions and discounts received.**
- The Special Provisions section adds: *"If lease is not completed according to the terms and conditions, Resident will be charged all applicable realtor/locator fees and repayment of all concessions received during lease term."*

**What this means:** If either resident breaks the lease early or gets evicted, they could owe back $8,397+ in concessions, plus the $2,799 reletting charge, plus any locator fees the property paid to Apt Insiders. This is standard for concession-heavy leases, but the exposure is significant — potentially $12,000+ in penalties on top of any unpaid rent.

**Recommendation:** Understand that the "3 months free" is not truly free — it's conditional on completing the full 15-month lease term.

## THINGS TO BE AWARE OF

### 1. 60-Day Notice Requirement
You must give **60 days' written notice** before the lease end date or before moving out during any renewal period. If you fail to give proper notice, even if you move out on time, you're responsible for **an additional month's rent**. This is more than the typical 30 days — mark the calendar for no later than May 8, 2027 to submit written notice.

### 2. Automatic Month-to-Month Renewal
The lease auto-renews month-to-month after July 7, 2027 unless proper 60-day notice is given. During month-to-month, rent can be increased with notice.

### 3. Joint and Several Liability (Paragraph 30)
Both residents are **jointly and severally liable** for all lease obligations. This means if one roommate doesn't pay rent, the other is fully responsible for the entire amount. If one roommate's guest causes damage, both residents are liable. This is standard but important to understand when signing with a roommate.

### 4. Reletting Charge = $2,799
If you break the lease early (move out without paying through the full term, fail to give proper notice, or get evicted), the reletting charge is **$2,799** — equal to one full month's rent. This is **in addition to** continued rent liability until a new tenant is found, plus the concession clawback described above.

### 5. Utility Costs Are Separate
You pay separately for:
- **Water & Sewer:** Allocated via sub-metering through Conservice (variable)
- **Electricity:** Direct to utility provider (you set up the account)
- **Internet & Cable TV:** Direct to provider
- **Trash, Pest Control, Amenity Fee:** Bundled as "Community Bundle" at a **flat $75/month**
- **Utility New Account Fee:** $20 (one-time)
- **Utility Final Bill Fee:** $20 (one-time, at move-out)

**True monthly cost estimate:** $2,799 rent + $75 community bundle + ~$50-80 water/sewer + electricity + internet = likely **$3,000-$3,100/month** all-in (during months when rent is being paid).

### 6. Personal Liability Insurance Required
You **must** maintain $100,000 personal liability insurance throughout the lease. Failure to maintain it is considered an "immediate, material, and incurable default" — meaning they can terminate your lease. The property must be listed as an interested party on the policy. Renter's insurance that includes liability coverage satisfies this.

### 7. No Packages Accepted by Office
The Package Acceptance Addendum explicitly states the office **does not accept packages** and will not be held liable for missing packages. Plan for Amazon lockers, package lockers on-site, or delivery instructions accordingly.

### 8. Parking
- Parking is managed by **Parkade** (third-party)
- Two vehicles are registered: 2025 Acura Integra (FL) and 2017 Ford Focus (FL)
- No parking fee is listed (appears to be free for registered vehicles)
- Vehicles must be registered with management
- Unregistered vehicles can be towed without notice

### 9. No Smoking — Anywhere
Smoking is prohibited inside all buildings **and** on balconies and patios. Violation is a material breach of the lease. This includes e-cigarettes, vaporizers, and hookah.

### 10. No Short-Term Subletting (Airbnb Prohibited)
Listing the apartment on Airbnb or similar platforms is explicitly prohibited and constitutes a material lease violation.

### 11. Move-Out Inspection
You have the right to be present for a scheduled mutual move-out inspection. **If you fail to attend, you waive the right to contest any damage charges.** Schedule this proactively.

### 12. Washer/Dryer Included at No Extra Cost
A full-size washer and dryer are included in the unit at $0/month. You're responsible for reporting any issues promptly — if a washer leak causes damage because you didn't report it, you're liable.

### 13. Animal Policy
No pets are occupying the unit per the signed addendum. If a pet is desired later, a new Animal Addendum must be completed and approved. The property allows up to 2 pets with an extensive breed restriction list (Pit Bulls, Rottweilers, German Shepherds, Chows, Akitas, Dobermans, Mastiffs, and many more are prohibited).

### 14. Homebody Rent Reporting
You're auto-enrolled in rent reporting to credit bureaus at **$9.95/month** (or $14.95 for both residents). This is **optional** and can be cancelled at any time. If you don't want it, cancel within the first month to avoid charges.

### 15. Holdover Penalty
If you stay past your move-out date, holdover rent kicks in at **125% of then-existing rent** on a daily basis, with no notice required. Don't overstay by even a day.

## TENNESSEE-SPECIFIC NOTES

- This lease includes the **Tennessee Uniform Residential Landlord and Tenant Act** provisions for counties where it applies (Davidson County/Nashville is covered).
- The waiver of notice of termination for non-payment of rent is standard in Tennessee — they can file a detainer warrant immediately without giving you a separate notice to pay or quit.
- Tennessee law allows landlords to charge up to 10% late fee, which this lease does.
- Security deposit of $300 is modest and standard. Tennessee requires return within 30 days of move-out with an itemized statement.

---

## BOTTOM LINE

This is a clean, standard Tennessee apartment lease with no red flags or unusual provisions. The main things to internalize:

1. **The concession clawback is real.** Breaking this lease early could cost $12,000+ between repaid concessions, reletting charges, and locator fees.
2. **60 days notice, not 30.** Mark May 8, 2027 on the calendar now.
3. **Both roommates are on the hook for everything.** Have an honest conversation about finances and expectations.
4. **Budget ~$3,000-$3,100/month all-in**, not $2,799.
5. **Get renter's insurance with $100K liability before move-in** — it's a lease requirement, not optional.
6. **Cancel Homebody Rent Reporting** if you don't want the $9.95-$14.95/month charge.
